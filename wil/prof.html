Brief work History

2005. Back to Spain back to Gaia
From 2003 to July 2005.Projects included the National Virtual Obvservatory and the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. In particular for SDSS developed CasJobs to handle the large volume of long queries on the multi terabyte dataset.
2000-2002 took a break from space and science and went to work for Laverock von Schoultz in sunny Gibraltar. Worked mainly on the multilingual XSLT soloution for Victor Chandler.
1996-2000 worked at European Space Reasearch & Technology Centre on serveral missions including : Hipparcos Integral Planck GAIA
1993-1996 worked at the European Space Operations Centre on Envisat/SCOSII and ATOS.

Current interests/projects


Worked on the initial prototype for the GAIA global processing with Lennart Lindegren. This work continues at University of Barcelona . The global solution for such a massive dataset (>100Tb) represents an interesting problem from both the Computer science and Astronomy perspectives. The original prototype is discussed in An OO framework for GAIA data processing . Currently this is being extended to a more general system to attempt processing simulated GAIA data (the real data will not be available for several years). Also worked on cost estimates etc. for the GAIA data processing centre with Michael Perryman.I shall now head one of the coordination units for the data reduction.
The NVO is an exciting project to make astronomical data available from disparate providers through a unified virtual interface. Worked on many NVO prototype services and the . Produced both Simple Image Access (SIAP) and Cone Search Services for at STScI. So far at JHU have worked on SIAP and OAI for Sloan (Open Archive Interface).
Also worked on the searchable registry and ADQL/SkyNode.

Sloan Digital Sky Survey / SkyServer

Skyserver is a magnificent tool for exploring the data from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. Have worked on the the new (DR2) batch query system with local storage and the site in general.
Behind the scenes revamped the FITS2JPG converter to use an ASINH stretch to produce better colour images. Also adapted it to produce gray scale images for each band.

Hierarchical Triangular Mesh

Have become the curator of the HTM at JHU. The HTM is a neat spherical partitioning scheme. Initially produced the Java version of the HTM library currently in use on GAIA. Intent to write a C# version also.
Produced a Java version of HEALPix, the paper Splitting The Sky: HEALPiX and HTM compares the two schemes.

Java - ShowSky - Hipparcos Animations

Have been developing Java applications for Astronomy since about 1996 with the JDK1.0. Many applets were written to present the Hipparcos data in different forms and may be viewed on the Hipparcos Website. Over 1999 - 2000 developed ShowSky for GSCII quality control at STScI (while working in Gibraltar).
