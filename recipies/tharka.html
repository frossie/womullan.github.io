Tharka (base for Indian dishes)

Home Page

Ingredients

1 onion
1 tin of tomatoes (optional)
1 tsp huldi (tumeric)
1 heaped tbsp garam masala
1 heaped tbsp curry powder
1/2 tsp salt
1/4 tsp jeera seeds (cumin)
6 cloves fresh garlic finely chopped
3-4 fresh chillies finely chopped
1 piece fresh ginger finely chopped
Coriander/Cilantro - finely chopped

Instructions

Chop onion finely. Place some sunflower/vegetable/olive oil in a pot and heat it - add the jeara seeds and then the onions. Fry onions well. Then add the ginger and garlic and cook for a little while (5 minutes). Next add huldi, garam masala, curry powder and salt - stir in. Now add the chillies.
You now have two choices:
This mixture is called tharka, into it you can add one of the following combinations stirring until cooked -
spinach & potatoes,
zucchini & potatoes,
green beans & potatoes,
cabbage (white) & potatoes.
Finally sprinkle some of the chopped coriander

OR

Alternatively you can add the tomatoes and continue to cook this sauce until most water is condensed out. To this you can add one of the following combinations until cooked- chicken and fresh cream/coconut milk, and/or potatoes cooked chick-peas & potatoes (and water if needed), cooked lentils such as mung daal (keep simmering for 20 minutes once tharka is added) cauliflower & potatoes, aubergine & potatoes. Finally sprinkle some of the chopped coriander
